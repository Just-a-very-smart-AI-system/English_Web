Learning hub

Take part in the Learning hub activities and community events to practise your English skills with learners from around the world.

Activate your English

Take part in the activities and practise your English skills by writing comments, recording yourself speaking and sharing photos with descriptions. You'll improve your language level by activating the English you know. You'll also learn by reading, listening to and replying to the contributions of other learners.

Current topics are facilitated, which means the LearnEnglish team educators will also be taking part and sharing their own thoughts, ideas and experiences.

New learning materials will be published regularly, so come back often to see what's new.

Topic

Home sweet home

Do you live in a flat or a house? Is it big or small, old or new? Do you like living there? In this Learning hub, we're talking about where we live, our homes.

In this topic you can:

  • listen to the LearnEnglish team talking about their homes, and then talk about your own home
  • read our descriptions of our dream homes and write your own
  • practise the names of parts of a house
  • learn the difference between house and home.

Tea or coffee?

In this topic, we're talking about tea and coffee! Do you prefer tea or coffee? What's the most popular hot drink in your country? Do you have a favourite mug, cup or teapot?

To participate in this topic, you can:

  • listen to people talk about their tea and coffee drinking habits, and tell us if you prefer tea, coffee or another hot drink
  • listen to people talk about favourite mugs, then share a picture of your favourite mug or cup and tell us about it
  • do a quiz about some expressions with tea or coffee.

Books and reading

In celebration of World Book Day on 23 April, this Learning hub topic is about books and reading. Even if you don't read much, there's something here for you!

There are lots of ways you can participate.

  • Watch a video of people from the LearnEnglish team talking about books they're reading and then tell us what you like about something you're reading.
  • Watch Jo and Kirk talk about what they like and dislike about the book or film versions of a story. Let us know what you think too!
  • Share your reactions to one of the short stories in our Story zone and discuss your ideas with each other.
  • Read descriptions of novels that the LearnEnglish team recommends to English learners.

Shakespeare and company

So, can you name many of Shakespeare's plays? Have you read any of them? Which other great writers do you know about?

To participate in this topic, you can:

  • listen to some of our colleagues talk about their favourite line from Shakespeare, and then tell us about yours
  • read about some more great writers and tell us about writers you like
  • find out about Shakespeare's life and works and tell us if you have a favourite Shakespeare play
  • do a quiz about language invented by Shakespeare.

Community Events

The language clinic: Live Q&A session

Join our live event and ask our expert educator your questions about the English language and learning English.

What is the event about?

  • Grammar: Tenses, conditionals, passive voice, etc.
  • Vocabulary: Synonyms, antonyms, word usage, etc.
  • Pronunciation: Common mistakes, tips for improvement
  • Idioms and Phrases: Meaning and usage.

Grammar lesson: Bucket lists – language to talk about dreams, hopes and wishes

Join our live event to support our learners studying grammar: Language to talk about dreams, hopes and wishes.

What is the event about?

  • Definition: A list of things you want to do before you die.
  • Group Activity: Create sentences for different scenarios.
  • Write Your Bucket List: List five items using the structures learned. Share with a Partner
  • Class Discussion: Share lists and correct errors. Q&A: Answer questions about grammar.

Writing workshop: How to write a great essay conclusion

Watch a recording of our live event to support our learners studying for English writing exams: How to write a great essay conclusion.

What is the event about?

  • Briefly introduce the workshop’s purpose. Explain why a strong conclusion is crucial for an essay.
  • Summarize the main argument without repeating it word for word. Connect the essay’s topic to a broader context or implications.
  • Show examples of effective and ineffective conclusions. Analyze what makes the good conclusions effective and the bad ones weak.
  • Participants write a conclusion for a provided sample essay or their own essay. Pair up and exchange conclusions for feedback.

Reading workshop: IELTS – True/False/Not Given questions

Watch a recording our live event to support our learners studying for English exams: IELTS Reading – True/False/Not Given questions.

To participate in this topic, you can:

  • Explain why mastering True/False/Not Given questions is crucial for the IELTS Reading section.
  • Identify keywords in the question and locate them in the passage. Focus on the text and avoid using outside knowledge.
  • Break into small groups and answer the questions together. Review answers as a class and discuss the reasoning behind each answer.
  • Allow time for participants to check their answers with provided solutions and explanations.
