Grammar for A1-A2

Adjective and Preposition: Explanation

Some adjectives go with certain prepositions. There are no grammatical rules for which preposition is used with which adjective, so it's a good idea to try to learn them together. To help you do this, write new vocabulary in your notebook in a sentence or phrase.

However, there are some patterns that can help you. Let's look at them first. Remember that a preposition is followed by a noun or a gerund (-ing form).

With at

He's really good at English.

She's amazing at the piano.

They're terrible at organising anything.

I'm not very good at drawing.

With about

We often use about with adjectives of feelings like angry/excited/happy/nervous/sad/stressed/worried, etc. to explain what is causing that feeling.

I'm angry about the decision.

He's nervous about the presentation.

She's excited about the new job.

They were worried about the exam.

With of

We can use to to show the connection between people or things.

He's married to the director.

I'm addicted to my phone.

I'm allergic to nuts.

It's similar to the old one.

Grammar for B1-B2

Adjectives: gradable and non-gradable

Do you know how to use adjectives in phrases like a bit cold, really cold and absolutely freezing? Test what you know with interactive exercises and read the explanation to help you.

It's really cold.

It's absolutely freezing.

This exercise is really difficult.

This exercise is completely impossible.

Gradable adjectives

Most adjectives are gradable. This means we can have different levels of that quality. For example, you can be a bit cold, very cold or extremely cold. We can make them weaker or stronger with modifiers:

She was quite angry when she found out.

The film we saw last night was really funny!

It can be extremely cold in Russia in the winter.

Non-gradable: absolute adjectives

Some adjectives are non-gradable. For example, something can't be a bit finished or very finished. You can't be a bit dead or very dead. These adjectives describe absolute qualities. To make them stronger we have to use modifiers like absolutely, totally or completely:

Thank you, I love it! It's absolutely perfect!

Their farm was totally destroyed by a tornado.

My work is completely finished. Now I can relax.

Exercises

Ex1

Read the following sentences and identify the non-gradable adjectives. Underline them.

  1. The room was completely empty.
  2. That was a perfect performance.
  3. The movie was absolutely amazing.
  4. She is a unique artist.
  5. The glass was totally full.

Ex2

Read the following sentences and underline the gradable adjectives.

  1. The weather is very cold today.
  2. She was quite happy with her results.
  3. The soup is too salty.
  4. He is rather tall for his age.
  5. The book was extremely interesting.

Grammar for C1-C2

Advanced passives review

Do you know how to use all the different forms of the passive? Test what you know with interactive exercises and read the explanation to help you.

Grammar explanation

Example

Aliya Monier directed the film.

The film was directed by Aliya Monier.

Be + past participle

Example

The new smoke alarm was installed yesterday.

The new smoke alarm was installed yesterday by the company director herself.

A podcast can be made with minimal resources.

New laws should be created to regulate electric scooters.

The impersonal passive

The impersonal passive is used with reporting verbs such as allege, believe, claim, consider, estimate, expect, know, report, say, think, understand, etc. It reports what an unspecified group of people say or believe.

It is estimated that millions of people visit the site every year.

It is believed that the walls date from the third century BCE.

It is reported that mosquitoes transmit the disease.

Millions of people are estimated to visit the site every year.

Exercises

Ex1

Complete the sentences with the correct passive form of the verbs in parentheses.

  1. The results ____________ (announce) at the conference next week.
  2. This song ____________ (play) on the radio all the time.
  3. A new shopping mall ____________ (build) in the town center.
  4. The culprit ____________ (identify) by the police soon.
  5. Many valuable artifacts ____________ (find) during the excavation.

Ex2

Complete the sentences with the correct form of "be" and the past participle of the verbs in parentheses.

  1. The letter ____________ (send) yesterday.
  2. The book ____________ (write) by a famous author.
  3. The repairs ____________ (finish) by the end of the week.
  4. The cake ____________ (bake) just now.
  5. The decision ____________ (make) by the board of directors.
